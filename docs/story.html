Story

The player has just been hired by an independent game publisher as a playtester for their upcoming narrative-adventure game, titled ___. The game is being worked on by an indie development studio made up of only six people. Moving to a new city, the player begins their new job as the playtester for ___ and builds friendships with each of their team members.

___ is being published by Nomina Games, a company known for publishing promising indie projects. After being introduced to their team members, the player soon meets the higher-ups of Nomina Games, including its CEO and its Head of Marketing.

As work on ___ progresses, it becomes more and more clear that some of the ones in charge of Nomina Games are more concerned about how well the game performs than they are with the game’s quality or what it represents, and have no regard for the work-life balance of their developers either. These details are exposed to the public when Nomina Games is outed as an immoral and corrupt company during a guest presentation at a local University.

Eventually, the team (and the player) must make a choice: Continue to rely on Nomina as a reliable publisher despite conflicting values, or abandon Nomina and self-publish the game, even though this would extend development time and require each team member to take a second job in order to both fund the game and make a living. They would also lose the rights to the name ___, as the name is owned by Nomina.

After carefully thinking it over, the team chooses to cut ties with Nomina. They will lose all funding for the project (and the naming rights?), but can now self-publish the game and no longer have to endure the poor working environment. The team can now work at their own pace and use whatever ideas they want. However, they are no longer backed by a publisher, so each team member must take a second job in order to keep the project funded. This caused development to take another whole year, but working on the game became significantly more enjoyable and the team’s relationships with one another strengthened as a result. When the game does eventually release, it does so to moderate success and a small but adoring audience. It is also given a new title: Everyday.

Setting

Where - Most scenes will take place in the office/studio where the team works, though there may be other locations as well. Everyday takes place in a city in the United States, but the exact setting will remain mostly ambiguous.

When - Everyday is set in the present day, specifically late 2023. I would like for certain relevant issues (such as artificial intelligence and its role in creative processes) to be topics of discussion amongst the characters.
